Natural beauty
& landscape

Sveti Filip i Jakov sits on a gentle arc of the Pašman Channel, where the mainland breaks into a scatter of green islets. Pebble coves give way to pine woods, the saltpans of Sv. Petar and lavender fields inland — a landscape built for slow mornings and long, gold evenings.

Cultural & historical heritage

First mentioned almost a thousand years ago, the town wears its history in stone — and keeps it alive. Local cultural-arts societies, the KUD-ovi, hold Dalmatian song, dance and costume at the centre of every feast, from klapa nights to the Vela Gospa procession.

Climate &
geography

The Mediterranean at its most even-tempered: hot, dry summers cooled by the afternoon maestral, mild green winters, and a channel that stays calm and shallow. Set midway between Zadar and Biograd and sheltered by the island of Pašman — which is exactly why the sailing, swimming and sunsets are so kind.
